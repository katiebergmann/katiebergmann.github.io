    The Odyssey book cover and posterPoster: 20” x 40” Mixed Media: Paper, Foam, Photoshop & Illustrator (2015)

    The Odyssey has been a book that has always created a lot of vivid, visual interest for me growing up and when I was given the chance to re-illustrate novels for my Senior Exhibition project, I jumped at the opportunity to recreate scenes from Homer’s most classic novel. My vision was to depict a minimalist and originial interpretation of Scylla, a Greek mythlogical sea monster who lived on one side of a narrow channel opposite of Charbydis. Originally constructing the outline from points and paths in Illustrator, I cut each scale out of a deep-blue metallic paper and hand-laid each piece usings foam pop dots and adhesive to create a three dimensional appearance. The overall image was broken into 5 segments before I photographed them and ran them through both Photoshop and Illustrator to create my final image. While I exhibited the design as a large movie-sized poster, it can also be applied to a book cover as well. My goal was to make this design one that can stand on its own as both a poster but also be visually interesting for a book cover as well.

  • Dialogue Poster
    x Close

    Whitworth Senior Exhibition PosterPoster: 11” x 17” Illustrator (2015)

    This poster was created for Whitworth Universities Senior Exhibition: Dialogue. Each of the artists featured in the senior exhibitions main goal was to create something visually stimulating to the viewer, to convey a message, and evoke some sort of response or emotion from the viewer. Therefore the most basic concept behind “Dialogue” is that there is a conversation between two or more people. In this circumstance, the conversation is between the artists and the viewer of the exhibition. My goal was to somehow illustrate this concept without using the typical cliche imagery one might think of when hearing the word “dialogue” (ie: talking, people, words, etc.). For me, when I think of dialogue, I think of connections and how conversation brings people together so I chose to go abstract in design and create an abstract mesh made of lines and circles in order to illustrate “connections”. The background is a made-from-hand low poly art design that enhance the abstract mesh in both visual interest and in concept. The postcard is simply the negative version of the poster. The deep blue colors were chosen due to the sight location which consist of several red, brick walls around campus - in this way, the colors will make the poster stand out against the wall and other posters that surround it.

  • Japantown
    x Close

    Japan Celebration Poster11” x 17” Mixed Media: Origami Paper, Ink, Paint, Photoshop & Illustrator (2014)

    This design was created as a celebration poster for San Fancisco, California’s 108th Anniversary - the oldest and one of the last remaining Japantowns in the United States of America. Before this project, I had primarly done all of my designs using only computer software and Adobe programs, so I wanted to incorporate several various mediums in order to make this poster highly unique. My inspiration was derived from both classical Japanese art as well as the landscape. In order to construct this image, I cut out the landscape by hand from traditional and authentic Japanese origami paper, crumpled each piece to add texture and then scanned it into the computer. Photoshop was used to manipulate each piece so that they would fit evenly together and to add dimension into the landscape image. Underneath the landscape is a simple silhouette image of the San Francisco skyline and it even features the famous five-tiered Peace Pagonda that is unique to SanFran’s Japantown. The Japanese symbol in the upper right hand corner represents the number 108 in relation to Japantown’s 108th anniversary and even doubles as a representation of the mountains in the landscape adjacent.

  • Tide Pods

    Tide Pod Bus Wrap11” x 17” Illustrator (2014)

    This illustration is a concept for an electric bus wrap design for the cleaning product - Tide Pods. Essentially the electric bus is a better, cleaner, and more innovative means of transportation in the similar way that Tide Pods is a “cleaner, greener” means of cleaning since it is a detergent, stain remover, and brightener all-in-one minus the harmful chemicals most detergents have. The theme of the bus is centered around one of the three scents: Ocean Mist. The tide pods stand in for possible fish that would typically be in an ocean scene and are involved with a clean, clear, and bright environment enhancing the idea that Tide Pods are a product that are clean, bright, and efficient just like the electric bus.

  • Wine Label
    x Close

    Customized Wine Label2 “x 8 1/2” Mixed Media: Paper, Ink, Photoshop & Illustrator (2014)

    This design is meant to be a wine label wrap for a mead (honey wine) bottle. I chose to create an entirely made up brand in order to test myself with how well I can do designing something from thin air as opposed to working with a prompt. For this project, I centered my concept around mead - a honey wine made from clovers and honey, therefore I named my brand White Clover. I wanted my design to be classy so I stuck to a simple palette of matte black, white, and a prominent gold color in order to make this brand feel more high-end. The winery, Abeja Vineyards, was derived from the Spanish word “abeja” meaning honeybee in order to connect back to the importance of bees in the making of mead. The dates and locations were taken from my own life in order to make the design a little bit more personal - after all, this is a hypothetical design! I initally made the design on Illustrator, using Photoshop for the image of the bee, and then printed it. With a gold pen, I went back in and traced over the design and scanned it back into Photoshop to fix some mistakes. By doing this, it gives the designs a more three-dimensional presence. The biggest challenge of this design was to incorporate a standard-sized bar code and Surgeon General’s warning in a way that did not take over the design or distract the viewer to much. By using the white in other places, I believe it helps to hide the ugliness and the size of that portion of the label.

  • Valentines Day

    Valentine's Day Card5.5” x 8.5” Folded & Illustrator (2014)

    My concept behind this Valentine’s Day Card was in pursuit of articulating what “love” is and translating the universal truths of it within the human experience. A large portion of this project was centered around avoiding cliche imagery such as hearts, the color red and cherubs, as well as the love-puns that appear quite commonly among Valentine’s Day cards. The concept I decided to represent as a universal is the idea that “love grows”. Illustrated quite simply by the tree and the leaves moving outwards, the tree represents time and the idea of continuous growth, both factors in the loving relationships we have with others. I believe that details are what can make a good design great and therefore are extremely important in all of my work, therefore, I transferred the idea of the tree to the back of the card by integrating it into the bar code. Underneath the bar code, can be found the date for Valentine’s Day (a stand in for the code that would be there if actually created by a company). Even though I wanted to avoid all the V-day cliches, I still wanted to add a little something fun to this design to push the concept even further. So in the very center of the tree, there is a small heart worked into the shape of the tree rings - a little “Easter egg” for the viewer.

  • John Fluevog
    x Close

    Fluevog Soprano Carey Advertisment11” x 17” Mixed Media: Photography, Photoshop & Illustrator (2014)

    John Fluevog is known for his lines of progressive, unique, and art deco style of shoes and the Soprano Carey is no different. This design was constructed for a design brief surrounding the Soprano Carey. Since Fluevog prides itself on the uniqueness and attention-grabbing nature of this shoe, my advertisment design features the most unique and flamboyant of all animals, the peacock, who is seemingly hoarding multiple pairs of the Soprano Careys in its nest. Since the image of the peacock was constructed to be an illustration, I had to make the shoes fit into the illustrated environment. Using Illustrator and Photoshop, I designed a graphic image of the shoe derived from the original photo. This helped to incorporate the shoe to the environment and enhanced the idea that the Soprano Carey’s are “unique soles for unique souls”.

  • Chameleon Typography

    Typography Logo Design8.5” x 11” Illustrator (2014)

    The point of this project is very simple - to utilize both typeface and design in order to create in interesting logo. In these two images, I designed two animals that could potentially be part of a logo - a chameleon and a squirrel. I created these images using the typeface Curlz MT and every aspect of the animal is taken from part of the inital letter. For the chameleon, the eye and toes were taken from the baseline of the “C” while the chin of the lizard is from the beak. As for the squirrel, the ear and eye were taken from the end point of the tip of the “S” and the face, foot, and paw were taken from the baseline of the “S”.

  • Forever Stamp

    Celebrate Hispanic Culture Stamp7/8” x 1” and 8.5” x 11” Landscape: Photoshop & Illustrator (2014)

    Stamps help to illuminate what is valued in people and in their culture. In this case, hispanic culture is what is represented within this particular stamp. For this project, I tried to combine both American (since it is a US Forever stamp) and Hispanic culture in a way that would convey a balance. I did this by tracing Hispanic roots back to the Aztec and Mayan cultures - the ancient civilizations of the Hispanics, and drew from their art as inspiration for this stamp. By using imagery from American culture, the flag, and the traditional patterns of Hispanic art, I created an illustration of both cultures without one overpowering the other. The biggest challenge in this project was simply constructing an image that, when shrunken to the size of a stamp, would not lose its details and meaning.

  • Hosanna

    Whitworth Yearbook: Natsihi47p0 x 75p0 Photography, InDesign & Illustrator (2015)

    I was hired by the Whitworth University Yearbook staff to be the Graphic Design Editor for the book. My job was to utilize the photos, stories, font, and overall vision of the Head Editor and translate them into each spread. The vision of the Head Editor was to create a crisp and clean design that highlighted the images and stories in a way that hasn’t been done in the past 20 years. One of the main features that shows up in each spread is the integrated relationship of the text (mostly the header) within the dominant photo of the page in a unique way. Shown are three of the 200+ spreads I have done for the Natsihi each using the same fonts (DIN) but shaping each header in a different way in order to cater to the images chosen.

  • Big Cat Habitat

    Customized Business Logo Designs3.5” x 2” and 8.5” x 11” Photoshop & Illustrator (2014)

    This design is a mock-up of what a logo design might look like on various methods of advertisment such as a business card, a letterhead, or standard envelope. For this design, I created my own business - a wildlife rescue called Big Cat Habitat and constructed an image of a cheetah using vector paths in Illustrator. The challenge with this project was designing the image of the cat that would be understood in a variety of sizes and not look out of proportion if enlarged to a great extent or illegible if shrunken. I also wanted a logo that was understood just as well in black and white as in color so I designed two versions. To add interest to the design, I incorporate a yellow within one of the logo designs.
