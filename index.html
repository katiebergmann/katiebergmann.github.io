In this circumstance, the conversation is between the artists and the viewer of the exhibition. e goal was to avoid cliche imagery. inking about connections and how conversation brings people together, I chose to create an abstract mesh made of lines and circles in order to illustrate “connections”.

JAPANTOWN CELEBRATION POSTER

This design was created as a celebration poster for San Francisco, California’s 108th Anniversary. In order to construct this image, I cut out the landscape by hand from traditional and authentic Japanese origami paper, crumpled each piece to add texture and then scanned it into the computer. Photoshop was used to manipulate each piece so that they would t evenly together and to add dimension into the landscape image.

- Fall 2014
- Poster Design
- 11” x 17” Origami Paper, Photoshop & Illustrator

The Odyssey

My vision was to depict a minimalist and original interpretation of Scylla, a Greek mythological sea monster who lived on one side of a narrow channel opposite of Charybdis. Originally constructing the outline from points and paths in Illustrator, I cut each scale out of a deep-blue metallic paper and hand-laid each piece using foam pop dots and adhesive to create a three dimensional appearance.

- Spring 2015
- Book Cover & Poster Design
- 20” x 40” Paper, Photoshop & Illustrator

24 Blackbirds: Flat Package To-Go Box

A simple, eco-friendly design for a hip, "underground" bakery, 24 Blackbirds, known for their resourceful and environmentally-friendly materials. Since the packaging was constructed from recycled, bio-degradable and environmentally-friendly paper, the buyer can rest easy that their purchase contains deliciousness for them while simultaneously making the earth a happier, cleaner place.
